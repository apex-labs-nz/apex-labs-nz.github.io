Support

Support for SailNav and all Apex Labs software is handled directly by the people who build it — which means answers come from someone who knows the code and sails the boat.

Get help

Email support@apexlabs.co.nz and we'll get back to you as soon as we can. We're in New Zealand (NZT), and we answer most messages within two working days — often much faster, occasionally slower if we're on passage somewhere without signal, which we hope you'll agree is the right reason for a navigation app's support to be delayed.

Help us help you

For questions or problems with SailNav, the following makes a fast, useful answer much more likely:

  • Your device and iOS/iPadOS/tvOS version (e.g. "iPad Pro 13″, iPadOS 26").
  • The SailNav version, from the app's settings screen.
  • What you did, what you expected, and what happened instead.
  • If it involves boat instruments: how you connect (NMEA 0183 or Signal K) and what equipment is on the other end.
  • Screenshots, if the issue is visible.

Beta testing

During beta periods, SailNav is distributed through Apple's TestFlight. Beta feedback — including crash reports sent through TestFlight's built-in feedback tools — goes straight to the development build queue. Email ben@apexlabs.co.nz with the subject "SailNav" if you'd like to be considered for a beta place.

A note on navigation safety

SailNav is a planning and situational-awareness aid, not a certified navigation system. Always carry and consult official charts and publications, and never let any app override the judgement of the skipper. If you believe SailNav has shown you something misleading, we want to know about it urgently — that's the most important category of support email we can receive.
